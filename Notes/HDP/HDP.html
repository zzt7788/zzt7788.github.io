1. Preliminaries on random variables

  1. Basic quantities associated with random variables
    • \(\|X\|_{L^p}=(\mathbb{E}|X|^p)^{1/p}\) is a norm. Thus the space \(L^p(\Omega,\Sigma,\P)\) is a Banach space.
  2. Some classical inequalities
    • Integral identity. \(X=\int_0^{\infty}\P \{X>t\}dt\).
    • Markov’s Inequality. \(\P \{X\geq t\}\leq \frac{\mathbb{E} X}{t}\).
    • Chebyshev’s Inequality. \(\P \{|X-\mu|\geq t \}\leq \frac{\sigma ^2}{t^2}\).
  3. Limit theorem
    • Strong law of large number. $S_n/n , a.s. $
    • Central limit theorem.
      \(Z_n:=\frac{1}{\sigma / \sqrt{n}} \sum_{i=1}^n(X_i-\mu)\rightarrow N(0,1)\) in distribution.
    • Poisson Limit Theorem \(S_n\rightarrow Pois(\lambda)\) in distribution.

2. Concentration of sums of independent random variables

  1. Why concentration inequalities?
    • Tails of the normal distribution. \((\frac{1}{t}-\frac{1}{t^3})\cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-t^2/2} \leq \P \{g\geq t\}\leq \min \{1/2,\frac{1}{t\sqrt{2\pi}} \}e^{-t^2/2}\).
  2. Hoeffding’s inequality
    • Hoeffding’s inequality.
      \(X_i\) symmetric Bernoulli distribution, then for \(t\geq 0\), we have \[\P \{\sum_{i=1}^n a_i X_i\geq t \}\leq \exp(-\frac{t^2}{2\|a\|^2})\]
    • Hoeffding’s inequality two sides.
    • Hoeffding’s inequality for general bounded random variables.
  3. Chernoff’s inequality
    • Chernoff’s inequality
      \(X_i\) be independent Bernoulli r.v. , \(\mu=\mathbb{E} S_n\), then for \(t>\mu\), we have \[\P \{S_n\geq t\}\leq e^{-\mu} (e\mu /t)^t.\]
  4. Application: degrees of random graphs
  5. Sub-Gaussian distributions
    • Sub-Gaussian properties
      • The tails of \(X\) satisfy \[\P\{|X|\geq t\}\leq 2 \exp(-t^2/K_1^2).\]
      • The moments of X satisfy \[\|X\|_{L^p}\leq K_2\sqrt{p}, \,\,p\geq 1.\]
      • The MGF of \(X^2\) satisfies \[\mathbb{E} \exp (\lambda^2 X^2)\leq \exp (K_3^2\lambda^2), \,\, |\lambda|\leq 1/K_3.\]
      • The MGF of \(X^2\) is bounded at some point, \[\mathbb{E} \exp(X^2/K_4^2)\leq 2. \]
      • If \(\mathbb{E} X =0\), \[\mathbb{E} \exp (\lambda X)\leq (K_5^2\lambda^2).\]
    • Sub-Gaussian norm \[\|X\|_{\psi_2}:=\inf \{t>0: \mathbb{E} \exp(X^2/t^2)\leq 2.\]
  6. Genearl Hoeffding’s and Khintchine’s inequalities
    • Sums of independent sub-gaussians Let \(X_i\) be independent, mean zero, sub-gaussian r.v. Then \(S_n\) is also sub-gaussian and \[\left\| \sum ^{n}_{i=1}X_i \right\|_{\psi_{2}}^{2}\leq C\sum ^{n}_{i=1}\left\| X_{i}\right\| _{\psi_{2}}^{2}.\]
    • General Hoeffding’s inequality 1. \[ \P \left\{ \left| \sum ^{n}_{i=1}X_{i}\right| \geq t\right\} \leq 2\exp \left( -\dfrac{ct^{2}}{\sum ^{n}_{i=1}\left\| X_{i}\right\| _{\psi_{2}}^{2}}\right). \]
    • General Hoeffding’s inequality 2. \[ \P\left\{ \left| \sum ^{n}_{i=1}a_i X_{i}\right| \geq t\right\} \leq 2\exp \left( -\dfrac{ct^{2}}{K^2\|a\|^2}\right) \] where \(K=\max_i \|X_i\|_{\psi_2}\).
    • Centering If \(X\) is a sub-gaussian r.v. then \(X-\mathbb{E} X\) is sub-gaussian, too, and \[\pnt{X-EX}\leq C \pnt {X}.\]
  7. Sub-exponentional distribution
    • Properties
      • The tails of X satisfy \[\P \{|X|\geq t\}\leq 2 \exp (-t/K_1).\]
      • The moments of X satisfy \[\norm {X}_{L^p}\leq K_2p.\]
      • The MGF of \(|X|\) satisfy
        \[\mathbb{E} \exp (\lambda |X|)\leq \exp (K_3 \lambda), 0\leq \lambda \leq 1/K_3.\]
      • The MGF of \(|X|\) is bounded at some point, \[\mathbb{E}\exp (|X|/K_4)\leq 2.\]
      • If X is mean zero, then \[\mathbb{E} \exp (\lambda X)\leq \exp(K_5^2\lambda ^2), \,\, |\lambda|\leq 1/K_5.\]
    • Sub-exponential norm \[\pno {X}=\inf \{t>0:\mathbb{E} \exp(|X|/t)\leq 2\}.\]
    • Sub-exponential is sub-gaussian squared
      \[\pno{X^2}=\pnt{X}^2.\]
    • Product of sub-gaussians is sub-exponential \[\pno{XY}\leq \pnt{X} \pnt {Y}.\]
    • Centering \[\pno{X-\mathbb{E} X}\leq C\pno{X}.\]
    • Orlicz spaces
      • A function \(\psi : [0,\infty)\rightarrow [0,\infty)\) is called Orlicz function if \(\psi\) is convex, increasing, and satisfies \[\psi(0)=0,\quad \psi(x)\rightarrow \infty \text{\,as\,} x\rightarrow \infty. \]
      • Orlicz norm and Orlicz space \[\norm {X}_\psi := \inf\{t>0: \mathbb{E} \psi (|X|/t)\leq 1\}.\] \[L_\psi :=\{X:\norm{X}_\psi \leq \infty\}.\]
  8. Bernstein’s inequality
    • Bernstein’s inequality 1
      \(X_i\) independent, mean zero, sub-exponential r.v. Then \[\P \{|\sum_{i=1}^N X_i|\geq t\}\leq 2 \exp \left [-c \min \left(\frac{t^2}{\sum_{i=1}^N\pno{X_i}^2},\frac{t}{\max_i \pno{X_i}}\right)\right].\]
    • Bernstein’s inequality 2
      \[ \P \{|\sum_{i=1}^N a_i X_i|\geq t\}\leq 2 \exp \left [-c \min \left( \frac{t^2}{K^2 \norm{a}^2}, \frac{t}{K}\right)N\right],\] where \(K=\max_i \pno{X_i}\).
    • Bernstein’s inequality 3
      If \(|X_i|\leq K\), then \[\P\{|\sum_{i=1}^N X_i|\geq t\}\leq 2 \exp\left( -\frac{t^2/2}{\sigma ^2 +Kt/3}\right).\]

3. Random vectors in high dimensions

  1. Concentration of the norm
    Let \(X=(X_1,\ldots,X_n)\) be a random vector with independent, sub-Gaussian coordinate \(X_i\) that satisfy \(\mathbb{E} X_i^2=1\). Then
    \[ \norm{\norm{X}_2-\sqrt{n}}_{\psi_2}\leq CK^2, \] where \(K=\max_i\pnt{X_i}\) and \(C\) is an absolute constant.
  2. Covariance matrices and principal component analysis \[ \operatorname{Cov} =\mathbb{E} (X-\mu)(X-\mu)^T \]
  3. Isotropy
    • Definition \[ \Sigma(X) = \mathbb{E} X X^T =I_n. \]
    • Characterization of isotropy
      A random vector \(X\) in \(\R ^n\) is isotropic if and only if \[ \mathbb{E} \inn{X}{x}^2=\norm{X}_2^2, \text{ for all } x \in \R ^n \]
    • Let \(X\) be an isotropic random vector in\(\R ^n\). Then \[ \mathbb{E} \norm{X}_2^2 = n. \] Moreover, if \(X\) and \(Y\) are two independent isotropic random vectors, then \[ \mathbb{E} \inn{X}{Y}^2 = n. \]
